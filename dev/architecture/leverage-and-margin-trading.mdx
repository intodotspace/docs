Leverage multiplies exposure through margin trading on the CLOB. You borrow USDC from the platform to buy more shares than your collateral would normally allow.
## How It Works
Margin: \$100
Leverage: 5x
Position Size: \$500
Shares at \$0.15: 3,333 shares

If price → \$0.30: +\$500 profit (500% ROE)
If price → \$0.12: Liquidation triggered
## Key Parameters
- **Initial Margin (IM):** 20% (required to open position)
- **Maintenance Margin (MM):** 10% (required to keep position open)
- **Max Leverage:** 10x

## Position Sizing
$$ \text{Position Size} = \text{Margin} \times \text{Leverage}$$

$$ \text{Shares} = \text{Position Size} / \text{Entry Price} $$

**Example:**
**subheading or just bold??**
\$100 margin × 5x = \$500 position
\$500 / \$0.15 = 3,333 shares

## Liquidation Price
$$\text{Liquidation Price} = \text{Entry Price} - (\text{Margin} / \text{Shares})$$

**Example:**
Entry: \$0.15
Margin: \$100
Shares: 3,333
Liquidation: \$0.15 - (\$100 / 3,333) = \$0.12
If market price hits \$0.12, your equity equals maintenance margin and liquidation begins.

## Profit & Loss
PnL = (Exit Price - Entry Price) × Shares
$$
\text{PnL} = (\text{Exit Price} - \text{Entry Price}) \times \text{Shares}
$$

| Example (Win) | Value |
|----------------|--------|
| **Exit** | $0.30 |
| **Entry** | $0.15 |
| **Shares** | 3,333 |
| **PnL** | ($0.30 - $0.15) × 3,333 = **+$500** |
| **ROE** | $500 / $100 = **500%** |

**OR THIS**

Example (Win):
Exit: \$0.30

Entry: \$0.15

Shares: 3,333

PnL: (\$0.30 - \$0.15) × 3,333 = +\$500

ROE: \$500 / \$100 = 500%

Example (Loss):

Exit: \$0.10

Entry: \$0.15

Shares: 3,333

PnL: (\$0.10 - \$0.15) × 3,333 = -\$167

ROE: -\$167 / \$100 = -167%

Leverage amplifies both gains and losses proportionally.
